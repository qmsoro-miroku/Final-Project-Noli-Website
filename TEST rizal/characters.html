Characters

Main Characters

Crisóstomo Ibarra

Protagonist

Educated Filipino who returns from Europe to reform his hometown and honor his father’s memory.

María Clara

Love Interest

Symbol of purity and the beloved of Ibarra; her fate reflects the cost of colonial and clerical abuse.

Padre Dámaso

Friar

Arrogant Franciscan who abuses power; central to the injustice suffered by Ibarra’s family.

Elías

Ally

Mysterious boatman who guides Ibarra and represents the revolutionary path versus reform.

Supporting Characters

Kapitán Tiago

Wealthy Patron

Influential socialite and foster father of María Clara; chases favor from Church and state.

Pilosopo Tasyo

Philosopher

Town sage dismissed as a madman; voices reason and critique of superstition and tyranny.

Sisa

Mother

Tragic figure whose suffering mirrors the plight of the powerless under colonial rule.

Basilio & Crispin

Sons of Sisa

Choir boys whose fate exposes cruelty within church-controlled institutions.

Tiya Isabel

Guardian

Kapitan Tiago’s cousin; oversees María Clara’s upbringing and social duties.

Antagonists

Padre Salvi

Friar

Calculating priest who replaces Dámaso; manipulative and cold, exerts control over the town.

Alférez

Civil Guard

Commander of the Guardia Civil; constant power struggle with the friars.

Doña Victorina

Social Climber

Obsessed with Spanish affectations; comic yet biting critique of colonial mentality.

Doña Consolación

Cruel Matron

Wife of the Alférez; sadistic, especially toward Sisa and other townsfolk.

Others

Fr. Sibyla

Dominican

Influential priest who often opposes progressive ideas and Ibarra’s school project.

Victoria & Sinang

Friends of María Clara

Lively and supportive companions who reflect the warmth, humor, and loyalty of Filipino womanhood amid social constraints.

Lt. Guevara

Sympathetic Officer

Reveals truth about Don Rafael’s persecution, guiding Ibarra’s understanding.
