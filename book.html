About the Book

Overview

Noli Me Tangere (1887) exposes colonial abuses through the story of Crisóstomo Ibarra and the town of San Diego. Written in Spanish, the novel helped ignite Filipino nationalism.

Major Themes

  • Religion vs. Morality
  • Power, Corruption, and Social Hierarchy
  • Education and Enlightenment
  • Love, Identity, and Sacrifice

Structure & Style

63 chapters + epilogue; satirical tone; vivid symbolism (see Symbols & Cover). The style blends realism with sharp social critique.

Impact

Banned and censored under Spanish rule, but widely read. Its sequel El Filibusterismo deepens the critique and points to revolution.
