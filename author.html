About the Author

José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda

Born: June 19, 1861 – Calamba, Laguna

Died: December 30, 1896 – Bagumbayan, Manila

Occupation: Novelist, Poet, Ophthalmologist, Reformist

Early Life & Education

Born to a wealthy family, Rizal was a child prodigy who spoke 22 languages. He studied medicine in Madrid and became an ophthalmologist in Europe.

The Propaganda Movement

In Europe, Rizal joined reformists calling for equal rights and an end to friar abuses. He wrote for La Solidaridad.

Noli Me Tangere (1887)

Published in Berlin, the novel exposed corruption and hypocrisy in colonial Philippines. Dedicated "To my country."

Legacy

  • National Hero of the Philippines
  • Inspired the 1896 Revolution
  • His works are required reading
  • June 19 is Rizal Day
